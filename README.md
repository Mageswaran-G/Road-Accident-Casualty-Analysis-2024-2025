2. Road Type is the Single Biggest Factor

The type of road is the strongest predictor of casualty volume:
Single Carriageway: Accounts for a massive 309.7K casualties. This indicates that undivided roads remain the highest risk environment.

**Dual Carriageway:** 67.4K casualties.
**Roundabouts:** 26.8K casualties.

ðŸ’¡ **Conclusion: Actionable Insights for Safer Roads**

This analysis of 417,883 casualties provides a clear mandate for road safety efforts:

Shift Focus to Behavior: The majority of casualties occur in Daylight on Dry Roads. This strongly indicates that driver behavior (speed, distraction) must be the primary focus of prevention campaigns, rather than solely weather-related risks.

Infrastructure Priority: Single Carriageway roads are the biggest risk factor, accounting for the vast majority of incidents. Targeted infrastructure improvements and revised speed limits on these roads offer the greatest opportunity for casualty reduction.

**In summary**, this data-driven project provides the foundation for strategically allocating resources toward behavioral change and improving high-risk single carriageway infrastructure to save lives.
